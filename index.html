Years of rigorous training pushed me to my limits — endless exercises, simulations, learning to survive in the harshest conditions. Each day was a battle to prepare my body and mind for this moment.

We faced countless hazards: intense G-forces, violent vibrations, atmospheric drag, storms, and precise fuel calculations.

03 · TRANSIT
03

Space Travel
to Mars

Months of solitude drifting through the void, riding a Hohmann Transfer Orbit. Tiny thruster burns adjusted our trajectory while our life support recycled oxygen, water, and food. Microgravity threatened bone and muscle — exercise was mandatory.

Isolation tested our minds, but onboard AI assistance helped manage communications delays, food supplies, and emergency scenarios.

01
ATMOSPHERIC ENTRY
Heat shield @ 1500°C
▶
The spacecraft slams into the thin Martian atmosphere at 5.4 km/s. The heat shield glows at over 1,500°C — hotter than the surface of the sun. This phase lasts only 4 minutes.
02
PARACHUTE DEPLOY
Supersonic at Mach 1.7
▶
A 21-metre supersonic parachute deploys at Mach 1.7 — still travelling faster than sound. The shock of deployment generates forces 9× the craft's weight. Mars' thin air barely helps.
03
POWERED DESCENT
Retro rockets + Sky Crane
▶
The heat shield drops. Radar locks onto the surface. Retro rockets fire to slow descent. The sky crane lowers the lander on cables — then cuts them and flies away. Pure autonomy.
04
TOUCHDOWN
Confirmation +22 min delay
▶
Contact. But Earth won't know for another 22 minutes — the time it takes radio signals to cross space. Mission control either celebrates history or mourns in silence. There is no in-between.
⚠ AUTONOMOUS PHASE — NO EARTH CONTROL
04 · LANDING
04

7 Minutes
of Terror

The infamous '7 minutes of terror' — everything happened autonomously. Heat shield glowing at 1500°C, supersonic parachutes, and retro rockets lowered us carefully. One miscalculation and it would have been catastrophic.

Communication lag meant Earth could not guide us — we relied entirely on onboard systems.

I turned back to look at my footprints…

They were gone.

The horizon… shifted.

My voice didn't sound like mine.

Something was breaking.

Not Mars.

Not space.

just   Me.

I woke up.

No countdown.

No Mars.

Only Me

and the sound of my own breathing.

It took me a second to understand.

I never left.

I never even got close.

The sky looked the same.

But I wasn't.

Some journeys don't happen in reality.

But they stay with you… like they did.

I didn't reach Mars.

But for a moment… it felt like I did.
